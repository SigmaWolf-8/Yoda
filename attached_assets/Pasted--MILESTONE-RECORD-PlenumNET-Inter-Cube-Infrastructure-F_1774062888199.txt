## Architectural Differentiation: Beyond VPN

A conventional VPN creates a point-to-point encrypted pipe between two machines. It is plumbing — it does not know what is inside the packets or where they should go. A central server routes all traffic, and if that server fails, the network stops. The Inter-Cube infrastructure replaces this model entirely with geometric routing: the topology itself is the protocol.

### Geometric Self-Routing

Every node receives a unique address in the 13-dimensional ternary hypercube. Each cube knows its 26 geometric neighbors, determined by mathematics, not by a central authority. Packets are forwarded along dimensional axes toward any destination. No central router exists or is needed.

### Cryptographic Identity

Node identity is not credential-based. Each node's PT26-DSA keypair is derived from a master secret, and keys rotate automatically on radian-epoch intervals (every 14 days). A VPN issues a shared secret or certificate; Inter-Cube provides a mathematically-bound identity tied to the node's address in the hypercube.

### Post-Quantum Tunnels

The CON (Cube Overlay Network) derives tunnel keys using TLSponge-385, providing 385-bit post-quantum security. Conventional VPNs rely on RSA or ECDH, both of which quantum computers will break. Every tunnel in the Inter-Cube mesh is quantum-resistant by construction.

### Self-Healing Fault Tolerance

The FTS monitors all 26 neighbors continuously. When a neighbor goes suspect or down, the GLB (Geometric Load Balancer) reroutes traffic through alternative dimensional paths automatically. The d! structural resilience property guarantees that the network tolerates dimensional failures without losing connectivity.

### Massive Scale

A single CRS node manages an address space of 1,594,323 vertices (3¹³). The Cubes-of-Cubes architecture scales further — each vertex can itself be a 13-dimensional cube, yielding up to 20.7 million post-quantum tunnels across the network.

### Automatic Key Rotation

Tunnel keys refresh on 14-day radian-epoch intervals without manual intervention. No administrator action, no certificate renewal process, no key distribution ceremony. The epoch schedule is mathematically defined and enforced by the protocol.

### WebSocket Relay Protocol

The WebSocket relay eliminates the need for port forwarding or publicly routable endpoints. After HTTPS registration, each cube opens a persistent WebSocket connection to the CRS relay, authenticates with its ternary address and public key, and exchanges wire protocol messages through the relay. The CRS forwards messages between connected nodes in real time.

**Connect:** Open WebSocket to `wss://plenumnet.replit.app/ws/relay`

**Authenticate:** Send `{type:"auth", address, publicKey}` → receive `auth_ok` with peer list

**Relay:** Send `{type:"relay", to:<target_address>, msgType:<wire_msg>, payload:<hex>}` → CRS forwards to target

**Keepalive:** `{type:"ping"}` → `{type:"pong"}`

**Offline queuing:** Messages to offline nodes are queued (up to 100) and delivered on reconnection

### Network Topology

The laptop registers with the CRS over HTTPS, providing its public key and endpoint. The CRS assigns ternary address `1111111111112` and returns its 26 geometric neighbors. After registration, the laptop opens a WebSocket to the relay and authenticates. Heartbeats are sent every 30 seconds to maintain the registration; the CRS tracks last-seen timestamps and marks nodes as live, stale, or dead.

Two-node relay is fully operational: Node A sends a message addressed to Node B, the CRS relays it through the WebSocket, and Node B receives it instantly with delivery confirmation. No port forwarding, no router configuration, no publicly routable endpoint required.
