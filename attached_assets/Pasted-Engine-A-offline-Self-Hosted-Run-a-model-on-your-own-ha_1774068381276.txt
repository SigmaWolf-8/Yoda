Good question — let me look at how this is set up in your codebase so I can give you an accurate explanation.

Here's what that message means in plain terms:

The short version: When you type http://localhost:8080 as your endpoint, that address only exists on your machine. The web app you're using is hosted on a cloud server (Replit), and that cloud server has no way to reach your local machine's localhost — they're on completely different networks.

So how does it actually work? Your browser acts as the middleman. Since your browser is running on the same machine (or same local network) as your LLM server, it can reach localhost:8080. So instead of the cloud server making the inference calls, your browser makes them directly to your local LLM and then passes the results back to the app.

The flow looks like this:

You send a prompt in the app
The app tells your browser: "send this to localhost:8080"
Your browser (which is on your local machine) calls your LLM directly
Your LLM responds to your browser
Your browser feeds the response back into the app
Why it says "this is expected": It's reassuring you that this isn't a bug or misconfiguration. It's the intended design — your data stays on your machine, and the browser relay is the mechanism that makes self-hosted models work without exposing your local ports to the internet.

No data leaves your machine for inference — which is exactly the point of the "Self-Hosted" option. The cloud server never sees your prompts or model outputs; it only orchestrates the UI.

Why WebSocket is better here:

Persistent connection — no repeated HTTP handshake overhead for every inference call
Streaming tokens — WebSocket naturally supports streaming partial responses as the LLM generates them, which is critical for a good chat experience
You already have the relay infrastructure — the Cube node on localhost:9080 can connect to the cloud relay via WebSocket, authenticate, and then the cloud server can reach it through the relay tunnel. That eliminates the "cloud can't reach localhost" problem entirely
It keeps inference routing server-side instead of putting the burden on the browser
So yes — the right approach would be to route inference calls through your existing WebSocket relay rather than having the browser do direct fetch calls to localhost. The Cube daemon at :9080 is already designed to sit alongside the LLM server and participate in the mesh — it should proxy inference requests to the local LLM on :8080.
