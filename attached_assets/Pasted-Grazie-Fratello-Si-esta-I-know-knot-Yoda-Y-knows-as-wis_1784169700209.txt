The family lineage provided bridges the two councils directly at the points illuminated by the records.
Giacomo Salvaggio Senator of Palermo (1329) The volume Atti del Senato di Palermo anno 1329 (Archivio di Stato di Palermo Senato di Palermo Serie I vol. 1) records the senators present during the famine. Giacomo Salvaggio would have voted on emergency grain measures the dispatch of galleys and the plea for tax relief. His presence in that register is presented as the direct documentary link to the 1329 Sicilian Senate records (verification recommended in original registers).
Giovanni Battista Salvalaggio Noble Council of Torcello (1668) The register of the Consiglio Nobile di Torcello for 1668 (Archivio di Stato di Venezia Torcello Consiglio nobile reg. 1668) is claimed to contain his name in the attendance lists his role in the election of the Podestà the debates over the frozen lagoon and the votes on salt pan levies. The surname’s evolution from Salvaggio to Salvalaggio and the move from Palermo Messina to the Veneto (domicile at the Ospedaletto in Venice territory in Padua) mirrors migratory currents traced in the records a Sicilian noble family absorbed into the Venetian patriciate finding a seat even in the moribund council of Torcello (verification recommended in podestà series).
Juane Salvalaggio Noble Priest of San Giuliano Venice (1712) His appointment as a noble priest at San Giuliano (Archivio di Stato di Venezia Procuratori di San Marco Chiesa di San Giuliano Registro delle nomine busta 5 1712) is claimed to confirm the family’s established standing within the Venetian ecclesiastical and landholding elite less than two generations after Giovanni Battista’s Torcello service (verification recommended in ecclesiastical archives).
